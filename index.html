More Information About Each Bill:

The USA FREEDOM Act:

The bill is co-written by the original author of the Patriot Act, Jim Sensenbrenner, and is meant to close the loopholes that has allowed the NSA to surveil millions of americans.

- Amends Section 215 of the Patriot Act - which is used to collect the phone records of almost every American every day – so that it can no longer be used in such a sweeping fashion.

- Limits collection of data: The secret FISA court would still be able to issue subpoenas, but they would be limited to collecting things that directly pertain to a terrorist, his associates, or his activities.

- Prevents alternative ways of bulk collection: The bill would requires the above standard for national security letters and pen registers, two other Patriot Act tools used to access Americans' records.

- Make changes to the FISA Amendments Act, the sweeping 2008 law that codified the warrantless wiretapping program. It would insert a very important restriction that would prevent the government from searching through FAA-collected data for U.S. person data in the absence of an emergency or a court order.

- Creates a special advocate in the FISA courts and adds new transparency requirements.

You can read the full text of the bill here.

While the bill is not perfect - it doesn’t prevent surveillance of millions of non-US citizens around the world - it’s a great start.

The Intelligence Committee Bill

This bill, which amounts to a “The Friends of the NSA Act,” is written by leaders of the House and Senate Intelligence Committees, who have on numerous occasions said that the existing collection of millions of call records by the NSA is legal, lawful and necessary.

While the bill hasn’t been published in full yet, we have been given many clues about what it’ll contain:

- Will grant the NSA explicit authority to spy on millions of americans, by gathering records listing the numbers, duration and time of all U.S. telephone calls [1].

- Will add “safeguards” and “limits” to who could search the data collected by the NSA, but would continue to allow them to continue mass surveillance of Americans [2].

- Will not end mass surveillance: Diane Feinstein has said of collection of call records: "I will do everything I can to prevent this program from being canceled" [3].
